Chest

The chest muscles are divided into three groups based on muscle fiber directions. Upper chest fibers go from high to low. In constast, lower chest fibers go from low to high. The middle chest fibers contribute to horizontal movements of the arm.

Back

The trapezius functions to elevate the scapula. Infraspinatus is one of the muscles of the rotator cuff which helps in stabilising the shoulder. The teres major is a thick muscle which along side latissimus dorsi draw the previously raised arm downwards and backwards. Rhomboids pull shoulder blades together. The erector spinae group straighten the back and provide side-to-side rotation.

Shoulders

Anterior deltoid muscle assists the upper chest muscle in flexing the shoulders. The lateral deltoids help to raise the arms by abducting the shoulders. In constrast to the anterior deltoids, postorior deltoids act with the latissimus dorsi exteranlly rotate the arms and produce its extention.

Biceps

The biceps act as elbow flexors and a supinators. The long head and the short head have different attachments and fiber directions. The short head is attached to the head of the humerus bone while the long head is attached to the shoulder. The brachialis has a very crucial role in the width of the biceps and assists in elbow flexion.

Tricpes

The lateral and medial heads of the triceps are only involved in elbow extension. However the long head is attached to the scapula and varies in tension depending on the position of the scapula.

Legs

The rectus femoris has an attachment to the hip and is a weak heap flexor. Vastus lateralis and medialis muscles extend from the head of the femur to the knee and therefore act as knee extensors. The hamstring muscle group in the back of the leg are involved in knee flexion.
